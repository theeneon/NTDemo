04 · Combat rules

Deterministic enough to test and replay.

The browser presentation will consume events from these rules; it will never calculate outcomes itself.

Turn orderLowest next-action time

Speed schedules each unit on a deterministic timeline; slot order resolves exact ties.

TargetingSkill selector, then stable ties

Legal targets are filtered first; percentage health and formation slot settle defaults.

ResolutionInteger, seeded outcomes

Damage, healing, loot, and any chance rolls use explicit rounding and one seeded RNG.

Safety100-turn maximum

A turn cap produces a controlled defeat and diagnostic summary instead of an infinite battle.

Battle setup and action order +

Each side enters with exactly four units in stable slots 0–3. A unit’s first action is scheduled at 1000 ÷ effective speed; after acting, the same interval is added again. Lower timestamps act first. Exact ties resolve by higher effective speed, then player before enemy, then lower slot.

The action sequence is: start-of-turn effects → defeat check → stun check → skill selection → target selection → costs/cooldown → effect resolution → passives → end-of-turn duration ticks → victory check.

Skills, cooldowns, and targeting +

Skills have an ordered AI priority, legal target selector, potency, effects, and cooldown. A cooldown of N blocks that skill for the next N owner turns; blocked counters decrement after each owner turn, including a stunned turn.

Default attacks choose the living enemy with the lowest health percentage, then front-most slot. Heals choose the living ally with the lowest missing-health percentage. If no legal skill target exists, the unit tries the next skill, then its basic attack.

Damage, healing, statuses, and defeat +

Damage is max(1, round(attack × potency% × 100 ÷ (100 + defense))) after explicit modifiers. Healing is based on source attack and potency, rounded once, and capped at maximum health. Health never drops below zero.

Damage-over-time and healing-over-time tick at turn start. Other durations tick at owner-turn end. Reapplying the same named status keeps the stronger magnitude and longer remaining duration; differently named modifiers stack additively. Stun consumes the turn and never stacks above the longer duration.

Passives, victory, and rewards +

Passives resolve after the triggering effect in stable order: active unit, allied slots, then enemy slots. Each passive can trigger once per source event. A 50-trigger chain guard cancels further triggers and records a diagnostic event.

A battle ends when one side has no living units. Simultaneous defeat is a draw and grants no completion reward. Victory grants the encounter’s fixed currency and squad experience plus seeded loot; first-clear rewards apply once. All four selected ninjas receive squad experience, including defeated units.
